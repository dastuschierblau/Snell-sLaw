Snell's Law

n1sin(theta1) = n2sin(theta2)

Snell's law describes how light is refracted when passing from one medium into another.

When light passes from a medium with a lesser refractive index into one with a greater refractive index, the light is skewed toward the normal line (line perpendicular to the substance boundary). Additionally the speed of light is reduced.
